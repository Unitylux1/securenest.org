Understanding Apps to Create NFT Art 

In today’s digital world, creating and collecting non-fungible tokens (NFTs) has become increasingly popular among artists, investors, and collectors alike. 

NFT art is a type of digital art that uses blockchain technology to ensure its uniqueness and ownership.

NFT creator apps provide users with an easy-to-use platform for designing their one-of-a-kind digital masterpieces. 

These apps often come equipped with features such as built-in graphic design elements, customizable templates, or even advanced AI algorithms that help generate unique pieces based on user input.

Using these applications can help both experienced and amateur artists create visually appealing digital artwork while also ensuring the piece is securely registered on the blockchain as an NFT.

Understanding how these apps work helps newcomers confidently navigate through the process of successfully minting their first tokenized masterpiece without feeling overwhelmed or daunted by technical jargon.

Features to Look For to Create NFTs

Creating NFTs can be a fun and lucrative venture for crypto beginners, but it’s essential to know the key features that make an NFT creator app outstanding.

 If you are looking to create free NFTs, you should also keep in mind that most of the apps that you can use have free accounts. 

If you want to do more advanced stuff, you will need to pay a subscription. Here are some of the crucial characteristics to consider when choosing an NFT maker:

  1. User-friendly interface:
    A beginner-friendly design will allow new users to navigate the app more easily and create their digital art with no hassles.
  2. Multiple platform compatibility:
    Choose an app that supports various blockchain networks, including Ethereum, Binance Smart Chain, and others, giving you flexibility in creating and trading your NFTs.
  3. Digital wallet integration:
    Seamless integration with popular digital wallets like Metamask or Trust Wallet is crucial for managing your NFT creations and transactions.
  4. Diverse templates and customization options:
    A robust selection of pre-designed templates and the ability to change elements such as color schemes, text styles, etc., will help you create unique art pieces tailored to your preferences.
  5. Rarity levels:
    Some apps offer rarity level settings, allowing you to categorize your artwork by rarity levels (common, uncommon, rare) giving it additional value for collectors.
  6. IPFS storage support:
    InterPlanetary File System (IPFS) provides decentralized storage for your artwork, ensuring its persistence even if the hosting server shuts down.
  7. Royalty management:
    Look for a creator app that allows you to set up royalties so you can receive a percentage of each subsequent sale of your artwork on secondary markets.
  8. Integration with popular marketplaces:
    Ensure that the app allows easy listings on prominent NFT marketplaces like OpenSea or Rarible so you can showcase your work more effectively among potential buyers.

By focusing on these features when selecting an NFT creator app, you’ll be well-equipped to create exceptional digital art within the thriving world of non-fungible tokens.

The Best NFT Creator Apps [Free] for 2023 

Discover the top NFT creator apps that are free to use in 2023, including Fotor, OpenSea, GoArt, NFT Creator, and SketchAR, each with their unique features and pros and cons.

Fotor 

Fotor is an outstanding online photo editor app for creating and one of the top free NFT creator apps for Android in 2023. 

With a user-friendly interface and diverse set of tools, Fotor allows even crypto beginners to create high-quality digital art pieces and turn them into NFTs seamlessly.

What sets Fotor apart from other platforms is its unique Rarity Builder feature that adds extra value and distinction to generated NFTs. 

For example, upon uploading artwork or images onto Fotor’s app, users can experiment with backgrounds or customizable elements, such as avatars provided within the platform.

The process not only enhances creativity but also offers an opportunity for artists to elevate their work before joining the growing NFT craze. Its app also allows you to make your NFT art on your phone as well.

OpenSea 

OpenSea is the largest and most popular marketplace for buying and selling NFTs. 

As a crypto beginner, OpenSea is an important platform to know about if you’re interested in investing in or creating NFTs.

The platform offers a wide range of digital assets, including art, gaming items, and virtual real estate. OpenSea also has its own mobile app available on iOS and Android devices for easy browsing and purchasing on-the-go.

OpenSea also allows you to create NFTs for free or buy NFTs from the market. OpenSea provides endless opportunities to explore the world of NFTs at no cost for sign up.

GoArt 

GoArt is a popular NFT creator app that offers an easy and affordable way for digital artists to create, mint, and sell their own NFTs. 

The app uses the latest AI technology to transform images into unique artwork pieces and allows users to add their own finishing touches before turning them into NFTs.

GoArt has received high ratings from both App Store and Google Play users.

Pixel Brush

Pixel Brush provides a way for digital artists to create NFT art in the popular pixelated look and turn it into valuable digital assets. 

This creator app allows users to mint unique, one-of-a-kind digital art pieces that can be sold or traded in various NFT marketplaces. 

It is not only specifically designed to make your own custom NFT specifically in pixelated form , but is also one of the best apps for creating NFTs. If you are looking for the best NFT app to use as pixel art editor, you can’t go wrong with this one.

With these tools at hand, creators can experiment with various formats of artwork, such as pixel art or traditional graphic design, before converting them to NFTs.

SketchAR 

SketchAR is an innovative NFT creator app that allows users to draw their own non-fungible tokens. 

This app is only available on mobile devices and offers a unique approach to creating NFTs, different from other apps that convert existing images into NFTs.

What sets SketchAR apart from the rest is its ability to enhance an image and turn it into an original piece of digital art with creative twists, perfect for turning unique concepts into extraordinary NFTs.

Notably, this app also comes with its own marketplace platform where artists can sell their creations as one-of-a-kind digital assets.

FAQ

How Do I Create an NFT Image for Free?

Creating an NFT image for free is possible by using various NFT marketplaces such as OpenSea, Rarible, SuperRare, and Nifty Gateway. 

These platforms offer unique features and allow you to upload your digital art for others to purchase as unique tokens.

However, each platform has its own rules and fees that you must comply with before listing your work. For instance, some platforms require creators to have their digital wallets or cryptocurrency, while others don’t.

Can I Turn Any Image Into an NFT?

Yes, you can turn any image into an NFT by minting it on a blockchain platform. 

This process tokenizes the image and establishes ownership using cryptocurrency, such as Ethereum.

However, not all images may be accepted for minting due to copyright issues or other regulations set by NFT platforms. Some platforms also have specific file format requirements or size limitations for uploaded artwork.

It’s important to research and choose a reputable NFT marketplace that aligns with your goals and aesthetic before uploading your art piece.

What is the Minimum Cost to Create NFT?

Creating an NFT can range from being completely free to costing a significant amount of money. 

The cost depends on various factors, such as the platform used, the size and complexity of the artwork, and whether any additional services are required.

For example, some blockchain platforms may charge small fees for mounting NFTs or selling them in their marketplace, while others may charge higher initiation fees or require payment in cryptocurrency.

Creators may need to factor in costs, such as hiring graphic designers or coders to create their artwork if they lack those skills themselves.
