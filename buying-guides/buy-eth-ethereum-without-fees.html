How Can I Buy ETH Without Fees?

Understand the Fees Involved in Buying Ethereum

To buy Ethereum, it’s important to understand the different fees involved, including exchange fees, network transaction fees, and validator fees.

Exchange Fees 

One of the primary costs you’ll encounter when buying Ethereum is the exchange fee. This fee varies from one platform to another and essentially compensates the exchange for facilitating your trade.

For example, let’s say you want to buy Ethereum on a trading platform like Kraken, Binance or Coinbase, some of the popular cryptocurrency exchanges. They all charge a trading fee for purchases made via bank transfer or debit card transactions.

So, if you’re planning investing in Ethereum, you will have to pay these fees on every exchange. You could also buy crypto on a P2P-exchange, where you directly trade with people. Here you have the possibility to save these fees, as there are no trading fees on these platforms.

The disadvantage is that you need to find a person you can trust and who won’t try to scam you. In this regards, crypto exchanges are safer so the fees are fair compared to the safety you get.

Network Transaction Fees 

Network transaction fees, often referred to as “gas” in the Ethereum ecosystem, play a crucial role in ensuring your transactions get processed on the blockchain. These fees act as compensation for the validators who confirm and validate each transaction.

As a crypto beginner looking to buy Ethereum without paying high fees, it’s important to understand how these costs can impact your investments. Network transaction fees fluctuate based on various factors such as network congestion and demand for processing power.

During periods of high demand or increased traffic, gas prices may rise significantly â€“ making it more expensive to transact at that particular time. To minimize this expense while still securing your ETH purchase promptly, carefully monitor gas price trends by utilizing online tools like Etherscan’s Gas Tracker or Ether Gas Station before initiating any transactions.

Validator Fees 

Validator fees, also known as gas fees, play a critical role in the Ethereum network. These fees serve as an incentive for stakers to validate and confirm transactions on the blockchain.

These are fees that are always included in a transaction and which you cannot omit. As much as you try to optimize fees, these fees will always be paid. The only thing you can do is to optimize them as well.

However, validator fees can sometimes be quite high due to increased demand or network congestion. It’s important to keep in mind that these costs fluctuate based on various factors like recent transaction volume and overall usage of the Ethereum platform.

For example, during periods of high trading volume or widespread adoption of decentralized finance (DeFi) applications running on Ethereumâ€™s smart contracts, gas fees tend to rise significantly.

Other Ways to Buy Ethereum With Low Fees in 2023

Utilize decentralized exchanges, use limit orders when trading, join loyalty programs, monitor gas prices and adjust transaction fees accordingly, and choose exchanges with low trading fees to buy Ethereum with low fees.

Utilizing Decentralized Exchanges 

Decentralized exchanges (DEXs) are an excellent option for those looking to buy Ethereum with lower fees and more privacy. Unlike centralized exchanges, DEXs operate on a peer-to-peer network where buyers and sellers can trade directly without intermediaries.

Creating an account on them eliminates the need for transaction fees typically charged by centralized exchanges for cryptocurrency trading. Especially Ethereum is one of the biggest cryptocurrencies and offers many ways to buy it on different exchanges and brokers.

An example of a popular DEX is Uniswap, which operates on the Ethereum blockchain and allows users to exchange Ethereum and other ERC-20 tokens in a trustless manner. Another benefit of using DEXs is that they offer greater security since funds are stored within external wallets rather than being held by a third-party exchange.

Using Limit Orders When Trading 

One way to reduce fees when buying Ethereum is by using limit orders. A limit order allows you to set a specific price at which you want to buy Ethereum, and the trade will only execute if the market price reaches that level.

For example, let’s say you want to buy 10 ETH, but you don’t want to pay more than $2,000 per coin. You could place a limit order for 10 ETH at $2,000 each, then wait for the market price of Ethereum to hit that level before your trade executes.

By doing this, you can potentially save on trading fees and get more value out of your investment in the long run.

Joining Loyalty Programs 

Another strategy to potentially reduce fees associated with buying and staking Ethereum is to join loyalty programs offered by cryptocurrency exchanges. Some platforms offer rewards for their users, such as reduced trading fees or even additional tokens.

For example, Binance offers a point system where users can earn rewards like discounts on trading fees by holding their native token, BNB.

By joining these loyalty programs and taking advantage of the benefits they offer, investors can potentially save money in the long run when buying and staking Ethereum.

Monitoring Gas Prices and Adjusting Transaction Fees Accordingly 

Gas fees can make buying Ethereum expensive, but monitoring gas prices and adjusting transaction fees accordingly can help you save money. Gas fees fluctuate depending on network congestion and demand, so it’s essential to keep an eye on the gas charts before making a trade.

MetaMask has also enhanced its Gas UI to provide more accurate information and make it easier for users to adjust their transaction fees based on current market conditions.

BitGo wallets have a similar feature that allows users to lower their Ethereum transaction costs by adjusting the gas price and avoiding network congestion.

Choosing Exchanges With Low Trading Fees 

One good strategy to buy Ethereum with low fees is to choose exchanges that offer lower trading fees. Some popular exchanges like Binance, Bitfinex and Kucoin charge relatively low trading fees compared to other platforms.

It’s important to keep in mind that while it may be tempting to opt for the cheapest exchange available, there are other factors to consider as well such as security features and reputation.

Tips to Buy ETH Without Fees 

Consider using peer-to-peer marketplaces, buying ETH in larger quantities, trading with other cryptocurrency assets, exploring alternative payment methods, and researching promotions or discounts to buy ETH without fees.

Utilizing Peer-to-Peer Marketplaces 

Another way to buy Ethereum without paying high fees is through peer-to-peer marketplaces. These are online platforms that connect buyers and sellers directly, allowing you to purchase ETH from other individuals rather than a centralized exchange or broker.

Some popular P2P marketplaces for buying Ethereum include LocalEthereum, Paxful, and CryptoLocally. When using these sites, it’s important to research the seller thoroughly before making a transaction and use payment methods with built-in buyer protection like PayPal or credit cards.

Trading With Other Cryptocurrency Assets 

Another strategy for buying Ethereum with low fees is by trading other cryptocurrency assets. Many exchanges offer a variety of cryptocurrencies to trade, allowing buyers and sellers to swap one digital currency for another without the need for traditional fiat currency.

This method can be especially useful when you already own another cryptocurrency asset and want to exchange it for Ethereum. For example, if you have Bitcoin, you can easily trade it on an exchange like Binance or Coinbase Pro for Ethereum at a lower transaction fee than exchanging fiat currency like USD or EUR.

Considering Alternative Payment Methods 

Another way to potentially avoid fees when buying Ethereum is by considering alternative payment methods. Often, these payment methods like PayPal or wire transfer offer discounts like lower withdrawal fees.

For example, some crypto exchanges allow users to buy Ethereum using PayPal or other crypto wallets. Additionally, peer-to-peer marketplaces may provide the option to buy ETH using gift cards or even physical cash in person.

Before choosing an exchange or platform, it’s important to compare available payment options and associated fees to find the best fit for your needs.

Researching Promotions or Discounts 

It’s always a good idea to keep an eye out for promotions or discounts when buying ETH. Many exchanges offer rewards or bonuses for new users, such as free ETH for signing up or referral programs that give you and your friend a bonus when they join.

For example, eToro often offers promotional deals like commission-free trading periods or deposit bonuses. Coinbase also regularly offers rewards and bonuses for new users who sign up through referral links.

It’s important to note that while these promotions can be helpful in reducing costs, it’s still essential to do thorough research before choosing an exchange based solely on its promotional offerings.
